Okafor v. United States

Background. Petitioner Okafor, a citizen of a country experiencing civil unrest, sought asylum and withholding of removal. The immigration judge denied relief, finding the testimony not credible. Petitioner timely sought review.

Question presented. The issue is whether the agency's adverse credibility determination is supported by substantial evidence in the record.

Discussion. We review the agency's factual findings for substantial evidence. The record contains credible and compelling corroboration of past persecution. The government's contrary reading is unpersuasive and conclusory.

Conclusion. For the foregoing reasons, the petition for review is granted and the matter is remanded. It is so ordered. Marsh, Circuit Judge.
