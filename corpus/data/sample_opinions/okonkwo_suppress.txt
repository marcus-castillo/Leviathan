United States v. Brennan

Background. Defendant Brennan was charged following a search of his vehicle. He moved to suppress the evidence, contending the officers lacked a valid basis for the search and seizure under the Fourth Amendment.

Question presented. We must decide whether the search was supported by a valid warrant and reasonable suspicion.

Discussion. The officers possessed a valid warrant supported by probable cause. Defendant's argument is meritless and unsupported by the record.

Conclusion. Accordingly, the motion to suppress is denied. We affirm. It is so ordered. Okonkwo, District Judge.
